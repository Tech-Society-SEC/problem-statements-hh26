# Explanation

The solution must enable individuals to verify, own, and selectively share their financial identity credentials across institutions, eliminating redundant KYC, reducing onboarding friction for legitimate users, and establishing cryptographically verifiable proof of identity that is resilient against AI-generated spoofing and synthetic identity attacks.

## The Solution Should

- Detect and defend against AI-generated deepfakes, biometric spoofing, and injection attacks during identity verification and video KYC workflows
- Allow users to hold a portable, tamper-proof verified identity credential that can be shared with regulated entities on a consent basis, aligned with the DigiLocker and Account Aggregator framework
- Ensure that re-KYC and cross-platform onboarding leverage prior verified credentials without exposing raw PII (Personally identifiable information), using privacy-preserving techniques such as zero-knowledge proofs or selective disclosure
- Incorporate real-time anomaly detection to flag synthetic identity patterns, mismatched biometrics, and suspicious onboarding behaviour
- Be inclusive, accessible, and operable across low-bandwidth and low-literacy contexts consistent with RBI's financial inclusion mandate
