# Explanation

## The Problem

India's health insurance system has a two-sided failure: fraudulent claims get paid, and legitimate ones get blocked. Both happen because today's claim processing is reactive — built on rigid rule sets and post-settlement audits that are too slow to stop fraud in time, yet blunt enough to catch genuine patients in the crossfire.

A patient hospitalised for a real surgery shouldn't have their claim delayed for weeks, rejected on a false flag, or buried under re-verification requests because they share a billing pattern with a fraudulent provider. Yet this is routine. Insurers, lacking real-time intelligence, compensate for poor fraud detection by applying broad suspicion and genuine claimants pay that cost.

Meanwhile, coordinated fraud — duplicate billing, ghost procedures, inflated diagnostics across provider networks — slips through because it's only visible in aggregate, across hundreds of claims, over months. By the time the pattern is caught, the money is already gone.

## Your Challenge

Build a system that intercepts fraud before the payout and fast-tracks genuine claims with confidence, so that the right decision is made at the moment of submission, not weeks later in a recovery audit. Legitimate patients should never be the collateral damage of a fraud problem that better systems could have solved upstream.
